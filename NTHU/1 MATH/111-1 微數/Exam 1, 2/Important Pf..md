[[3-3 MVT#^be015b|The Mean Value Thm]]
Let $g(x)=f(x)-[\dfrac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}(x-a)+f(a)]$
	$\implies$ $g$ is continuous on $[a,\,b]$
		  $\land$ $g$ is differentiable on $(a,\,b)$
		  $\land$ $g(a)=g(b)$
		 $\implies$                            { Rolle's Thm }
		 $\;\exists\,c\in(a,\,b)$ s.t. $g'(c)=0$
